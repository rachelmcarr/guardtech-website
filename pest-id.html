Not Sure What You're Dealing With?

Browse common household and yard pests below. If you're seeing something and want it gone, request a free quote and we'll take it from there.

Occasional Invader

Bats

Roosts in attics, chimneys, and eaves, often unnoticed until droppings or noise gives it away.

Crawling

Bed Bugs

Hides in mattress seams and furniture during the day, feeding on people at night.

Stinging

Bees

Beneficial pollinators that build nests in wall voids, trees, or man-made structures.

Occasional Invader

Beetles

A range of species that wander in from the yard, especially in fall.

Crawling

Crazy Ants

Nests in massive numbers and moves in fast, erratic trails rather than straight lines.

Occasional Invader

Earwigs

Common under mulch and siding; usually a sign of excess moisture.

Stinging

Fire Ants

Builds dome-shaped mounds in open yard areas and delivers a painful sting when disturbed.

Crawling

Fleas

Jumps rather than flies, and multiplies quickly in carpets, pet bedding, and shaded yard areas.

Rodents

Mice

Leaves small droppings and gnaw marks, often heard scurrying in walls or ceilings at night.

Yard Pest

Mosquitoes

Breeds in standing water and is most active around dawn and dusk.

Rodents

Rats

Larger and bolder than mice, often leaving gnaw marks and burrows near foundations.

Crawling

Roaches

Fast-spreading and resilient, often found near food and water sources in kitchens.

Occasional Invader

Silverfish

Thrive in humid areas like bathrooms and basements.

Crawling

Spiders

Mostly harmless, but a sign of other insect activity nearby.

Wood-Destroying

Termites

Feeds on wood from the inside out, often going unnoticed until damage is already done.

Stinging

Wasps

Builds papery nests in eaves and attics; more aggressive than bees when disturbed.
